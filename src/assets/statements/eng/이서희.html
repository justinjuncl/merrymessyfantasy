"Everything that disappears leaves a trace. The question is what remains when everything is gone.

Therefore, we can think that everything that has disappeared continues to live secretly and exert an insidious influence."

Jang Baudrillar [Pourquoi tout n'a-t-il pas deja disparu?]

Old buildings and vacant lots that disappear quickly ... Buildings that are built high as if their mushrooming overnight would cause them to enter unfamiliar spaces. Within a few years, the landscape of the neighborhood that has been viewed for 15 years in the name of new city development is changing day by day. The alleys, which used to be a little dirty and winding, have been replaced with straight roads, and the low hills where trees and vines lived in tangles have turned into a forest of huge buildings. The fear of unfamiliarity felt in a familiar space is now transforming into a landscape that can only be seen in any city through an uncanny state.

I am now experiencing Uncanny Valley.

The act of compulsory viewing of a situation (at least I am), the space like the facade of a theater, and the city after Uncanny Valley approaching with a sense of loss ... I can't just beautifully record a proliferating city in a pattern of repeated circulation. Sometimes 00 times and sometimes 00 times (still undecided) I try to continue my work by accumulating cities and spaces.

Last summer,

I found a bee dead on a street.

Then I took a few steps further and saw a dragonfly dead.

As I entered the building and went up the stairs,

I saw several bees dead on the stairs.

I stood up and looked outside through a palm-sized window.

An illusion like a scene from a movie unfolds in front of me.

Huge cranes and gray buildings enter the reality.

This illusionary image enters my reality and is breaking it into pieces.

What is this ...

I began to raise questions right here.

Since then, I have a fear that my daily life may collapse.

This fear refers not to the fear of not knowing the future, but to the fear of knowing.

Knowing the situation that will come to me is identical to the fear (of chasing) of what is slowly approaching.

Pet bottles, plastic bags, disposable items ... The migration of civilizations built to be clean and convenient, on the contrary, erodes our daily lives. Climate warming, disease, starvation, and forest fires... It is a huge price that came back after removing daily inconvenience. Now you have to face discomfort. You have to stay in the negative.

So that fear becomes fear.

This is not a solution to a problem, but a fear of not being aware of the sense of fear that we should remain in us.
