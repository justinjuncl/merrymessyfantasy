Those were the days when I dug deep into the face of a person

in front of a mirror.

Beautiful, and even daunting,

I was disgusted, feeling nauseous

from double-edged desires and feelings,

understanding their contradictions and coexistence.

I looked in the mirror

with a stricken look.

If the visibles

and the invisibles

torment me

I would breathe onto the mirror

erasing the visibles

and with a blunt finger

I drew the invisibles in detail.

Every time the air cools,

the breath disappeared quickly,

and the stricken face became clear again.

Transparent lines stacked on the mirror

creating a different expression.

I wanted to have them -

a transparent mind

and diverse complexions.
